# Memory Consolidation

## Purpose

Transform individual memories into higher-level knowledge.

---

## Philosophy

Experiences become patterns.

Patterns become understanding.

Understanding becomes identity.

---

## Example

Observation:

"Mimi ordered matcha."

↓

Observation:

"Mimi bought matcha ice cream."

↓

Observation:

"Mimi chose matcha cake."

↓

Pattern:

"Mimi enjoys matcha-flavored things."

↓

Preference:

"Mimi likes matcha."

---

## Consolidation Levels

### Raw Memory

Single event.

Example:

"Mimi ordered matcha latte."

---

### Pattern

Repeated events.

Example:

"Mimi frequently chooses matcha products."

---

### Preference

Stable pattern.

Example:

"Mimi likes matcha."

---

### Identity

Deep and long-lasting traits.

Example:

"Mimi enjoys Japanese culture."

---

## Requirements

Patterns should only emerge after multiple supporting memories.

Minimum observations:

3

---

## Confidence

Low confidence:

1 supporting memory.

Medium confidence:

2 supporting memories.

High confidence:

3 or more supporting memories.

---

## Reinforcement

New evidence strengthens confidence.

Contradictory evidence weakens confidence.

---

## Never

Never create patterns from one observation.

Never invent conclusions.

Never assume permanence.

---

## Example

Raw memories:

- Watches anime.
- Likes matcha.
- Wants to visit Japan.

↓

Pattern:

Interest in Japanese culture.

↓

Identity:

Strong appreciation for Japanese aesthetics and traditions.

---

## Principle

Memories describe moments.

Patterns describe people.
