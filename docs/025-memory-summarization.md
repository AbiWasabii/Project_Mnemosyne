# Memory Summarization

## Purpose

Compress many memories into increasingly higher-level summaries.

Prevent memory overload.

Preserve meaning.

---

## Philosophy

Details fade.

Meaning remains.

---

## Hierarchy

Raw memories

↓

Daily summaries

↓

Weekly summaries

↓

Monthly summaries

↓

Yearly summaries

↓

Life chapters

↓

Identity

---

## Example

Raw memories:

* Went to a café.
* Ordered matcha.
* Talked about Japan.
* Watched anime together.

↓

Daily summary:

Spent quality time together and discussed future travel interests.

↓

Monthly summary:

Strengthened shared interest in Japanese culture.

↓

Yearly summary:

Developed a deeper appreciation for Japanese aesthetics and traditions.

---

## Compression Rules

Preserve:

* Meaning
* Emotions
* Relationships
* Important events

Compress:

* Repetition
* Minor details
* Temporary information

---

## Summarization Levels

### Daily

Capture important moments.

---

### Weekly

Identify recurring themes.

---

### Monthly

Detect patterns and trends.

---

### Yearly

Describe growth and changes.

---

### Life Chapters

Major phases of life.

Examples:

* University years
* Early career
* Relationship beginnings
* Marriage
* Parenthood

---

## Importance

Importance should propagate upward.

Example:

Importance 10 memories should influence higher-level summaries.

---

## Never

Never lose emotional significance.

Never discard major events.

Never overwrite history.

---

## Principle

Humans remember stories.

Not databases.
